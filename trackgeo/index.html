Background

Churchill Downs is an iconic horse racing venue located in Louisville, Kentucky, and is perhaps best known as the home of the Kentucky Derby. Founded in 1875, it's a symbol of both Southern tradition and the competitive spirit of horse racing. Its Twin Spires, an architectural feature that was added in 1895, have become a symbol of the racecourse and are instantly recognizable to fans of the sport.

Covering 147 acres, the racecourse features a one-mile dirt oval, and it also offers turf racing on the Matt Winn Turf Course. While the Kentucky Derby, a Grade I stakes race for three-year-old Thoroughbreds, is undoubtedly the star attraction, Churchill Downs hosts numerous other races throughout the year, including the Kentucky Oaks and the Breeders' Cup on occasion.
